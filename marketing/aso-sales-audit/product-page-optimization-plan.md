# Product Page Optimization Plan

Objective: use Apple's product page optimization workflow to test whether a different headline and screenshot order improves conversion before any pricing or packaging changes.

## Current Baseline

- 90-day App Analytics conversion rate: 0.7%.
- 90-day impressions: 65K.
- 90-day product page views: 3.32K.
- 90-day first-time downloads: 180.
- Product page source mix is search-led, which means the page copy has to do most of the selling.

## Hypotheses

1. A speed-first hero screenshot will lift conversion more than a generic utility-first hero.
2. A forms/spreadsheet-oriented sequence will outperform a feature dump.
3. A monetization-aware sequence that mentions pro packs, finance, and tax/tip will improve qualified conversion without hurting the top of funnel.

## Treatments

| Treatment | Core Message | Screenshot Order | Intended Audience |
|---|---|---|---|
| Control | Current English master | Current six-screen order | Broad traffic |
| A | Speed and forms | 1, 2, 4, 3, 5, 6 | Search-led users and office/work users |
| B | Power features | 4, 3, 5, 1, 2, 6 | Users who care about overlays, finance, and customization |
| C | Monetization-aware | 1, 2, 5, 3, 4, 6 | Users likely to understand the value of Pro Lifetime and Finance Pack |

## Success Metrics

- Primary: conversion rate.
- Secondary: product page views, first-time downloads, and IAP attach rate.
- Guardrail: do not reduce total downloads while lifting conversion.

## Launch Checklist

- Keep the control page untouched until the treatment is ready.
- Launch one treatment at a time in the highest-volume locale first.
- Measure long enough to account for weekly traffic variation.
- Compare against the current 90-day baseline rather than a single week.
- If a treatment wins, localize that winning structure before testing other copy.

## Recommendation

Start with Treatment A. It aligns best with the current search-led traffic and with the strongest screenshot concepts already in the repo.
