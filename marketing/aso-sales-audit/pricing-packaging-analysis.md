# Pricing and Packaging Analysis

## Current Package Structure

- `numpad.pro.lifetime`: the flagship one-time unlock for all packs and premium themes.
- `numpad.pack.finance`: a narrower entry point for finance-specific users.

## Revenue Snapshot

- 90-day IAP proceeds: $337.
- 90-day in-app purchases: 5.
- 90-day paying users: 2 daily average in the overview tile.
- 30-day sales proxy: $110 on the Trends page.
- Most 30-day sales are concentrated in USA and Canada, then Europe.

## What That Suggests

1. Monetization exists, but it is still thin relative to acquisition volume.
2. The lifetime purchase is likely doing most of the monetization work.
3. The finance pack is worth keeping as a lower-friction entry product.
4. Pricing changes should not come before page conversion improvements.

## Channel Notes

- iPhone drives most sales, so pricing and packaging should be optimized for the iPhone-first experience.
- Desktop sales are present but small, so they should not steer the package strategy.
- Search-led acquisition means the value proposition must be obvious before the purchase sheet appears.

## Recommendations

### Keep

- Keep `numpad.pro.lifetime` as the premium anchor.
- Keep `numpad.pack.finance` as the focused entry-level purchase.

### Test Later

- Test whether the finance pack should be presented earlier in the funnel for finance-intent traffic.
- Test whether the lifetime purchase should be framed as "all current and future packs" more prominently in the storefront copy.
- Test whether premium themes should be mentioned closer to the purchase prompt.

### Do Not Change Yet

- Do not change pricing until product page conversion is measured against a stronger baseline.
- Do not reorder public packaging without a test plan and explicit approval.

## Conclusion

The current package structure is sensible, but the app needs a stronger conversion page before pricing experiments are likely to pay off.
